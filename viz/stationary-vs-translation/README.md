# Stationary vs pure-translation SD-VAE latent contrast — translation IS observable

**TL;DR.** The controlled contrast **overturns the earlier "translation is geometrically
unobservable / below the noise floor" conclusion** (from `viz/signal-fsweep/`). When rotation is held
near zero, **pure-translation chunks change the SD-VAE latent ~2× more than stationary chunks**
(f=5: AUC 0.94; f=20: AUC 0.98). The signal forward motion needs is present — it just lives in the
**near-field floor** and is masked by two artifacts of the old `corr(|Δx|, latentL2)` statistic.

Generated by `external/nanowm/src/sample/stationary_vs_translation.py` against the Run 001 step-10K
checkpoint (used **only** for its frozen SD-VAE + config; previews any `f` without retraining), on the
**5 held-out val episodes**, `--seed 42 --n-batches 80`.

## Why the old metric (`corr(|Δx|, latentL2) ≈ 0`) was misleading

It pooled all chunks and asked for a Pearson correlation between `|Δx|` and latent change. That is the
wrong estimator here for two reasons:
1. **`Δx` is bang-bang** (0 or ~1.67 cm/chunk at f=5) — there is almost no within-"moving" variance for
   a correlation to latch onto.
2. **Pure-rotation chunks pollute it**: they have large latentL2 at ~zero `|Δx|`, dragging the
   correlation toward (even below) zero.

The right test holds rotation fixed and contrasts the two populations directly:
- **STATIONARY**: `|Δx| < 0.3 cm` AND `|Δθ| < 0.5°`  (the non-action noise floor)
- **PURE-TRANSLATION**: `|Δx| > 1.3 cm` AND `|Δθ| < 0.5°`  (driving straight)
- **PURE-ROTATION** [positive control]: `|Δθ| > 4°` AND `|Δx| < 0.3 cm`

## Numbers (seed 42, n_batches 80)

latentL2 = `‖z(k+f) − z(k)‖_F` in SD-VAE space (the quantity v-prediction is trained on).

| f  | stationary μ | translation μ | rotation μ | **signal/floor** | SNR (σ over floor) | **AUC** trans>stat | rot AUC |
|----|------|------|------|------|------|------|------|
| 5  | 12.0 | 23.5 | 38.5 | **0.96×** | 2.6σ  | **0.942** | 1.00 |
| 8  | 11.9 | 27.8 | 42.6 | **1.34×** | 3.8σ  | **0.964** | 1.00 |
| 10 | 11.9 | 30.6 | 44.4 | **1.57×** | 4.2σ  | **0.978** | 1.00 |
| 20 | 12.6 | 37.0 | 51.4 | **1.93×** | 13.7σ | **0.980** | 1.00 |

**f=10 is the sweet spot for retraining:** translation sits 1.6× over the floor (AUC 0.978), while
f=20 barely improves separability (0.978→0.980) for double the chunk duration (667 ms → coarser
control, worse Δy-negligible approximation, fewer decision points). See the Run 002 plan.

- **AUC** = P(a random translation chunk's latentL2 > a random stationary chunk's). 0.5 =
  indistinguishable; 0.94–0.98 = strongly separable.
- **signal/floor** = `(μ_trans − μ_stat) / μ_stat` — the action-attributable change relative to the
  non-action floor. **At the trained f=5 it is ~1:1 (marginal-but-real); raising f doubles it.**
- **Dose-response seals causation:** as f grows 5→20 (Δx 1.67→6.65 cm), translation's signal scales
  **monotonically** with displacement while the stationary floor stays flat (~12). A scene/content
  confound could not produce that.

## Spatial footprint (the heatmaps in `latent_compare.png`)

Each heatmap is the **32×32 SD-VAE latent grid** (= the camera FOV; top row = far field/horizon,
bottom row = near-field floor/robot body), showing **mean per-cell `‖Δz‖`** over all chunks of a class.
Panels 1/2/4 share an `inferno` scale; panel 3 is a diverging `translation − stationary` map.

- **Stationary**: uniformly dark — the floor. The dark wedge at bottom-center is the robot's own body
  (rigidly mounted → most static region; a registration sanity check).
- **Translation**: brighter, concentrated in the **near-field floor (bottom)** — parallax hotspots.
- **Translation − stationary**: positive (red) almost everywhere; the robot-body wedge is the only
  ~zero region (correctly — the body doesn't move relative to the camera).
- **Rotation [control]**: a *different* signature — brightest at the **top (far field/horizon)**,
  because yaw sweeps the distant background hardest.

⇒ The two motions paint opposite regions of the FOV, exactly as geometry predicts: **translation →
near-field floor; rotation → far-field horizon.** The "elevated camera de-magnifies forward motion"
claim holds only in the *far* field; the near-field floor carries a clear translation signal.

## Implication

The Run 001 action-branch failure (RMS 0.0088) is **not** an observability/camera problem — the signal
is in the latent. The likely causes are **(a)** the diagnosed checkpoint (step 10K = epoch 16) was deep
into overfitting (val bottomed ~epoch 3, no best-val checkpoint kept), and **(b)** at the trained f=5
translation's SNR over the floor is only ~1:1, easy for an overfit model to drop. Both are
**training-side, fixable without re-collecting data**. See `context/experiment-log.md`
(2026-06-02 stationary-vs-translation entry) and the Run 002 plan in `context/training-runs.md` /
`context/roadmap.md`.
